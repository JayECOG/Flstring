# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We, as contributors and maintainers of the `fl` library, pledge to make
participation in this project a harassment-free experience for everyone,
regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics,
gender identity and expression, level of experience, education,
socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion,
or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming,
and professional community.

---

## Our Standards

### Acceptable Behaviour

The following behaviours are expected of all participants:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language.
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints, experiences, and skill levels.
- Providing and accepting constructive feedback graciously.
- Focusing on what is best for the project and its users.
- Assuming good intent from other contributors.
- Acknowledging and learning from mistakes.
- Showing empathy and patience towards other community members.
- Keeping technical discussions focused and on-topic.

### Unacceptable Behaviour

The following behaviours are unacceptable in any project space:

- Personal attacks, insults, or derogatory comments directed at individuals.
- Trolling or deliberately inflammatory, provocative, or off-topic messages.
- Harassment of any kind, public or private.
- Publishing others' private information (addresses, contact details, etc.)
  without explicit permission.
- Sexual language or imagery, and any form of unwelcome sexual attention or
  advances.
- Sustained disruption of discussions, pull request reviews, or issue threads.
- Any conduct that a reasonable person would consider inappropriate in a
  professional setting.

---

## Maintainer Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this Code of
Conduct. They are expected to:

- Model the behaviour described in this document.
- Respond to reports of unacceptable behaviour promptly and fairly.
- Take appropriate and proportionate corrective action when warranted.
- Maintain the confidentiality of those who report incidents.

Maintainers have the right to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code,
issues, and other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct,
and may temporarily or permanently ban any contributor for behaviour deemed
harmful or inappropriate.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces, including:

- The GitHub repository (issues, pull requests, code reviews, commit messages,
  and discussions).
- Any project-affiliated communication channels.
- Public representations of the project (conference talks, blog posts, social
  media posts made in the project's name).

It also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in
public spaces.

---

## Enforcement

### Reporting

If you experience or witness behaviour that violates this Code of Conduct,
please report it by contacting the project maintainer directly via GitHub
([@JayECOG](https://github.com/JayECOG)) or through the contact method
specified in the repository's `SECURITY.md`.

All reports will be treated with discretion and confidentiality. Please
include:

- Your contact information (if you are comfortable providing it).
- A description of the incident, with as much detail as possible.
- Links to any relevant messages, pull requests, or issues.
- Any other context you consider relevant.

### Enforcement Process

Upon receipt of a report, the maintainer will:

1. Acknowledge receipt of the report within three business days.
2. Review the incident and investigate as needed.
3. Determine an appropriate response, which may include:
   - A private warning to the individual concerned.
   - A request for a public apology.
   - Temporary suspension of participation rights.
   - Permanent removal from the project.
4. Notify the reporter of the outcome, to the extent permitted by privacy
   considerations.

### Consequences

Unacceptable behaviour will not be tolerated. Consequences scale with the
severity and frequency of the behaviour:

| Severity  | Example                                   | Consequence                          |
| :-------- | :---------------------------------------- | :----------------------------------- |
| Minor     | Single instance of unhelpful language     | Private written warning              |
| Moderate  | Repeated minor violations, public insults | Temporary ban from participation     |
| Severe    | Harassment, threats, doxxing              | Permanent ban from the project       |

---

## Appeal

If you believe a moderation decision was made in error, you may appeal by
contacting the maintainer with a written explanation of your position. Appeals
will be reviewed and responded to within five business days.
